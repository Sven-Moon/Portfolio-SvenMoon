Project Overview

My first idea for an app was handed to me by a coworker who was running a side hustle of selling used phones. He needed a way to communicate the prices users could expect to get for a given phone in a given condition.

SellPhone fulfills that need. It also goes a little bit further by offering the ability to create a sales order that precedes the transaction.

As stated above, this was my first application, so it's riddled with inefficiencies and it probably comes across as pretty awkward, even as it fails to do anything of any serious complexity. BUT it works without a bunch of errors, despite it being my first attempt. So that's great.

After having not touched it for some time and having learned a great deal with respect to how I could improve it both in presentation and structure, I've decided to leave it untouched. I'd like to keep it preserved so I can appreciate how far I've come in the future, as I can when I go back and look at my 3rd grade writings I was once so proud of. Also it's a lot of effort I could put to better use elsewhere.

What I learned

Everything you see in this app is my first attempt. Okay, maybe I knew how to make a span of text bold before, but everything else was new, approached for the first time. I don't have anything to compare it to, but I was impressed at how accessible Angular is. The model makes sense. NgRx is ... powerful, but difficult to make use of when you don't first have a foundation in RxJs.

Certain complex tools like NgRx, I can say now that I didn't really "get" until later, so listing that is probably a little "optimistic," but it was used without crashing the site.
